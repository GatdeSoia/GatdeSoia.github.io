13 Rosas

A historical horror fiction about the terrors of fascism and the Spanish Civil War.

Developer: Achtung Autobahn. Status: Under Development. Planned release: TBA | Steam

What is 13 Rosas?

13 Rosas is a horror adventure game set in Spain during the ruling of its dictator Francisco Franco. Find the thirteen red roses and escape this nightmarish, impossible building full of ghosts and sorrow.

Conceived at first for my master thesis, the game is currently under development at our workers cooperative in Berlin, for which I'm the project lead and 3D artist.

The why for this game is simple: there is nothing scarier than fascism.

Historical horror fiction

The game is both an entertainment piece of media and a virtual memorial to the victims of fascism.

Engaging with the affordances of the horror genre, we are making a game that plays heavily on the metaphorical level, with monsters lurking around and an impossible architecture, but whose historical context is unfortunately tangible to this day.

References to the symbols of the resistance, its artists and poets, can be found scattered throughout the game, including the thirteen red roses that give name to this project, a group of women, members of the Unified Socialist Youth, that were incarcerated and executed by the fascist regime.

Background

The game was originally designed for mobile devices. I experimented with mixing the horror genre, a touchscreen interface, low-fidelity graphics and a topic that actually mattered to me. Also, not everyone has a gaming PC, but almost everyone has a smartphone, which was an important factor for introducing the game to as many people as possible, especially to the ones that still remember the dictatorship.

The resulting prototype is currently available in five languages: English, Spanish, Catalan, German and French.

But where ubiquity is high, discoverability is non-existent. The game has no chance to be found organically in the major mobile app stores. That is why we have switched development to PC and will be publishing the final game on Steam ‚Äìwith potential ports to mobile in the future.
