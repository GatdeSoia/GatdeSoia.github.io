of turning your passion

into your source of income


A message of hope for the artists in the crowd

and some cautionary tales about the consequences

of not taking care of your physical and mental health.

The bliss and curse of turning your passion into your source of income.
This is mainly a message of hope, but it comes with some responsibilities.
I will also show the link to this presentation if you want to come back to it later at the end slide.

DISCLAIMER

  • These are some of my conclusions after 7 years in the industry. Your experience may differ and so will your course of action.
  • I am no legal advisor nor a therapist.
"That's like... your opinion, man."
Little notice.
This is like, my opinion. I have a story, I have friends with stories. You don't get to do exactly what I did because these are the consequences of my seven years in the industry.
I just want you to listen to my pains, and the pains of others to identify the source of them and take action towards avoiding it for yourself and anyone that comes after.

THE *bliss*

THE JOY

  • Congratulations, your job doesn't suck!
  • The process is enjoyable.
  • The challenges are fun, or at the very least, exciting.
  • There's great satisfaction in creating things.
Me, rigging a ten-legged creature in Blender (the order of panels doesn't matter).

THE EXPERIENCE

  • Research phase for the next project can be a treat.
  • Learning never ends.
  • The more you do it, the better you get at it.
  • It is unavoidably an interdisciplinary craft.
Legit search.

THE FRIENDS WE MAKE ALONG THE WAY

  • Creative professions attract creative people (≠ like-minded people).
  • It's a collaborative process.
  • Your peers are in awe with your expertise the same way you are amazed by theirs.
  • You learn to appreciate more the things you do.
Watch Casablanca, I beg you.

  • Remember Gamergate?
  • Have a humble release, be looked down on.
  • Have a successful game, be harrassed and threatened.

  • "Why should I pay you if you like what you are doing?"
  • "Why should we give you money to make a game if you don't have the money to make a game?"

THE TAKEAWAY

YOUR JOB IS NOT YOUR LIFE

  • Work for living, don't live for working.
  • You don't need to hate your job to learn to separate.
  • Tell your boss I'm not allowing you to crunch.
In a fairer version of Modern Times, Chaplin's character would have had max five-hours shifts, a living wage and enough vacation days to disconnect from that mind-numbing job.

YOUR GAME IS NOT YOUR BABY

I am meant to be one with the sea.
  • Healthy habits come first.
  • Weekends are secularly sacred.
  • Holidays are not for personal projects.

MENS SANA IN CORPORE SANO

  • Eat healthily.
  • Do sports.
  • Sleep enough.
  • Get a standing desk and an ergonomic chair.
  • Block weekends, they belong to you.
  • Rely on friends.

WHERE DOES YOUR TIME GO?

Making/doing things:
Work.
Hobbies related to your craft.
Hobbies separated from your craft.

Meeting people:
Networking.
Meeting your industry friends.
Meeting friends outside the industry.

ORGANIZE

  • Join the union (Verdi).
  • Form a workers council.
  • Consider structuring your company as a co-op.
