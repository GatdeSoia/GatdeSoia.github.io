Flying Sheep Games

HTML5 original games.

Developer: Flying Sheep Studios.

From red pandas on hoverboards to sci-fi robot wizards

Between 2017 and 2020, I worked as a 3D artist for Flying Sheep Studios, a German game development company focused on HTML5 technology. In between the regular work-for-hire (consisting mainly on kid games for the giant German network Super RTL), the studio took the chance on creating their own games.

In the beginning, they served as low-time tasks to explore new formats and test technical constraints. I first started fixing environments for an existing three-lane racer called Red Panda: Surfer (optimizing polycount and UV space and redoing the textures to make them pop). For my last period at the company, I became the art director of Sunrise Roots, a cozy farming game, and one of the 3D character artists and the lead animator of Knights of Fortune, an ambitious mobile MOBA.

More than a 3D artist

Who knows how to use After Effects and has two thumbs? My previous life as a video editor came in very handy in my three years at the studio.

Flying Sheep's marketing department consisted on artists recording ingame footage and slapping the vanity plate on top, then sharing it in the company's social media accounts. This task would usually be assigned to one of the artists involved in the development of the game (either the UI/2D artist or the 3D artist). I enjoyed having this change of pace once in a while, and it wasn't specially disrupting, except for when we were preparing for conference showcases.

Another responsibility that gladly fell on my lap was to write and maintain the documentation for 3D art and audio pipelines. I did not make the calls for the music selection, but at least I managed to create an easy guide to keep the dBs at bay and avoid the major device-specific audio bugs.

And finally, I represented the company in a couple of occasions giving technical-artistic talks at game conferences around Europe. I took a liking to it, and to this day I still alocate time to giving talks and workshops on art and animation for video games.
